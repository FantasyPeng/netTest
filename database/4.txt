Yesterday the Prince of Wales and his sons walked through the trenches where Canadian and British forces fought and died during the Battle of Vimy Ridge. At a ceremony to mark the 100th anniversary of the battle on April 9, 1917, they were taken to the Allied front line, yards from the German lines in northern France.

The battle was a decisive victory for the Allies and featured four Canadian divisions fighting as the Canadian Corps for the first time.

The Prince of Wales led the tributes, describing the conflict as “the single bloodiest day in Canadian military history”, and calling the efforts of the soldiers an “an extraordinary example of selflessness for our future generations”.
